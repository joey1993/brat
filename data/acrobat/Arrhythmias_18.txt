The differential diagnosis includes sinus rhythm with first-degree atrioventricular block or atrial tachycardia with 2:1 atrioventricular conduction, with a second P wave concealed within the QRS complex. The latter diagnosis should be suspected whenever the P waves of a supraventricular tachycardia are midway between the ventricular complexes according to the Bix rule. 1
