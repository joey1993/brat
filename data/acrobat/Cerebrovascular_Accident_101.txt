History While at home in 1996, this 5-year-old, otherwisehealthy girl presented to a local hospital with sudden severe headache followed by seizures where neuroimages revealed a right frontal hematoma  caused by an AVM. She was referred to our service for further examination and treatment. Examination at the time of presentation The patient was fully conscious, the seizures were well controlled by anticonvulsants, and there was no motor weakness. Cerebral angiography demonstrated a S-M GI AVM in the right frontal area, which was fed by the anterior branch of the ascending frontal artery of the right middle cerebral artery (MCA) as well as by the orbitofrontal artery of the right anterior cerebral artery (ACA) ; and drained into the superior sagittal sinus (SSS) via cortical veins .
