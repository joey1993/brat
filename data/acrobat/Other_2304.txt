A woman with a history of one spontaneous miscarriage of 7-week gestation without curettage or other treatment had a vaginal delivery at 40 +4 weeks gestation. The placenta was retained in the cavity despite the use of oxytocics. Manual removal of the placenta was attempted, but unsuccessful. Emergency ultrasound scan revealed placenta tissue measuring 165 × 130 × 94 mm 3 without a clear boundary with myometrium. Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) confirmed placenta accreta the next day . Her pre-delivery hemoglobin concentration was 11.9 g/dL and the estimated blood loss was 200 mL without active hemorrhage.
