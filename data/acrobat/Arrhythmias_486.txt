A 24-year-old medical student was admitted to the emergency department due to palpitations triggered by emotional stress (after being on call for 24 h) without hemodynamic instability. The palpitations had begun 1 h prior to his arrival at the hospital. His blood pressure was 130/70 mmHg and no signs of heart failure were present. The 12-lead ECG showed a right bundle branch block (RBBB) wide QRS tachycardia with right axis deviation, an aspect suggesting fascicular VT . Intravenously adenosine and betablockers were ineffective in terminating the arrhythmia.
