A 75-year-old man was referred for treatment of severe mitral valve regurgitation. He had been receiving oral and inhaled corticosteroids for bronchial asthma and bronchial ectasia. Chest X-ray showed cardiomegaly with protrusion of the right atrium shadow and pulmonary congestion . Electrocardiography showed a normal sinus rhythm with counterclockwise rotation. Echocardiography revealed mitral valve prolapse on the medial side of the posterior mitral leaflet (including the posterior commissure) due to tendon rupture. Quantitative Doppler echocardiography indicated severe mitral regurgitation. Left ventricular function was maintained with an ejection fraction of 56%.
