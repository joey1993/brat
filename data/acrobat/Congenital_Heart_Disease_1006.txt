Proband (indicated by an arrow in the family tree of The proband was a 56-year-old man who suffered from a loss of consciousness while driving. At first, he was diagnosed with transient cerebral ischemic attack as the cause of syncope because he was temporarily paralyzed on the left side after the event. At the age of 59 years, he was first pointed out to have a QT prolongation and was referred to our hospital for evaluation of LQTS. His 12-lead electrocardiography (ECG)  displayed a marked QT prolongation (QTc: 597 ms), biphasic T waves in V4 and V5 and a heart rate of 51 bpm. He had no previous syncope except for that experienced during the traffic accident. There were no abnormal physical findings and no structural cardiac abnormalities on the ultrasound cardiography (UCG). His Schwartz scores were 4.5; QT prolongation, bradycardia, and syncope were observed, and he was therefore diagnosed Department of Cardiovascular and Respiratory Medicine, Shiga University of Medical Science, Japan and Center for Epidemiologic Research in Asia, Shiga University of Medical Science, Japan Received for publication Correspondence to Dr. Minoru Horie, horie@belle.shiga-med.ac.jpwith LQTS.
