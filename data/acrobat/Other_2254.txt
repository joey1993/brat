An apparently healthy 32-year-old male patient complained of reduced strength in his left hand. Over the past few months this phenomenon occurred especially and repeatedly when holding a frying pan, but there was no pain or weakness in any of the other muscles. He had two operations on his left middle finger at the ages of 10 and 18 due to a macrodactyly. Neurological examination gave evidence for a reduced sensitivity for touch in his left middle finger.
